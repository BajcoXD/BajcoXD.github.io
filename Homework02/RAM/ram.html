When choosing RAM, consider both speed (measured in MHz) and capacity (measured in GB) to match your system’s needs. For most tasks, 16GB of RAM is sufficient, but gamers, content creators, and multitaskers may benefit from 32GB or more. RAM speed, especially with DDR4 or DDR5, affects performance, particularly in gaming or intensive applications. If you're building a new PC, DDR5 is faster and more future-proof, but DDR4 remains effective and often more affordable. Make sure the RAM you select is compatible with your motherboard’s supported RAM type and speed. Dual-channel configurations (using two sticks) can further improve performance.
