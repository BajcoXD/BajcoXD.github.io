Choosing the right motherboard depends on your CPU and intended use. First, ensure compatibility with your processor—Intel and AMD CPUs require different socket types, like AM5 for modern AMD and LGA 1700 for Intel’s 12th and 13th Gen. Chipsets (e.g., B760, X670) affect features like PCIe slots, overclocking support, and USB ports, so pick one that suits your needs. For gaming and basic tasks, a mid-range chipset is often sufficient, while enthusiasts may want high-end models with more connectivity and expansion options. Also, check for RAM compatibility (DDR4 or DDR5). Opt for a well-reviewed brand with a reliable BIOS and solid build quality.
